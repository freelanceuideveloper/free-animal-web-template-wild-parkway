Amphibians

Discover animals that live both in water and on land, masters of transformation and adaptation.

About Amphibians

Amphibians are vertebrates that typically start life in water and later develop the ability to live on land. They have permeable skin and undergo metamorphosis, transforming from aquatic larvae to terrestrial adults.

Tree Frog

Colorful frogs adapted for arboreal life with sticky toe pads for climbing trees and plants.

Rainforests Vulnerable
Toad

Terrestrial amphibians with warty, dry skin that can secrete toxins for protection from predators.

Worldwide Stable
Salamander

Long-tailed amphibians capable of regenerating lost limbs, with some species being entirely aquatic.

North America, Europe Vulnerable
Poison Dart Frog

Small, brightly colored frogs whose skin contains potent toxins used by indigenous peoples on arrows.

Central/South America Near Threatened
Axolotl

Neotenic salamanders that remain aquatic throughout their lives, famous for their regenerative abilities.

Mexico Critically Endangered
Newt

Semi-aquatic salamanders that return to water to breed, with some species being highly toxic.

North America, Europe, Asia Vulnerable

Amphibian Marvels

Amphibians can breathe through their skin

Some frogs can freeze solid and survive

Axolotls can regenerate entire limbs

The largest amphibian can grow to 6 feet long
