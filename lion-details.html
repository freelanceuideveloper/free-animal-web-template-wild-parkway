African Lion

The King of the Jungle - powerful, majestic, and social big cats that rule the African savanna.

Overview

Lions (Panthera leo) are large cats native to Africa and India. They are the most social of all wild felids and live in groups called prides. Lions are apex predators and are considered a keystone species, playing a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of their habitat.

Scientific Classification
  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Class: Mammalia
  • Order: Carnivora
  • Family: Felidae
  • Genus: Panthera
  • Species: P. leo
Conservation Status
Vulnerable - Population declining

Lion populations have declined by approximately 43% over the past 21 years due to habitat loss and human-Wild conflict.

Physical Characteristics

Length: 1.4 - 2.5 meters (body), plus 60-100 cm tail
Weight: Males 150-250 kg, Females 110-180 kg
Color: Golden-yellow to light brown coat
Mane: Only males have manes, indicating maturity and health

Habitat & Distribution

Primary Habitat

Savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands

Geographic Range

Sub-Saharan Africa, with a small population in India's Gir Forest

Territory Size

Prides control territories ranging from 20 to 400 square kilometers

Climate Preference

Warm climates with temperatures between 20-30°C

Behavior & Social Structure

Pride Structure

Lions live in family groups called prides, consisting of 2-3 males, several females, and their cubs. This social structure is unique among big cats.

Hunting Strategy

Lionesses do most of the hunting using cooperative strategies. They hunt primarily at dawn and dusk, targeting large herbivores like zebras and wildebeest.

Communication

Lions communicate through roaring, which can be heard up to 8 km away. They also use scent marking, visual displays, and body language.

Conservation & Threats

Lions face numerous threats in the wild, leading to their classification as Vulnerable by the IUCN.

Major Threats
  • Habitat loss and fragmentation
  • Human-Wild conflict
  • Poaching and illegal trade
  • Prey depletion
Conservation Efforts
  • Protected area establishment
  • Community-based conservation
  • Anti-poaching initiatives
  • Breeding programs

Amazing Lion Facts

A lion's roar can be heard up to 8 kilometers away

Lions can run up to 50 mph in short bursts

A lion's mane gets darker as it ages

Lions sleep up to 20 hours per day

Female lions do 85-90% of the hunting

Lions have excellent night vision
