Reptiles

Meet cold-blooded vertebrates with scales, from ancient turtles to powerful crocodiles and slithering snakes.

About Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded vertebrates covered in scales or bony plates. They lay eggs on land and have adapted to diverse environments from deserts to rainforests.

Saltwater Crocodile

The largest living reptile, apex predators that have remained virtually unchanged for millions of years.

Southeast Asia Stable
Python

Non-venomous constrictors that kill their prey by wrapping around them and squeezing.

Africa, Asia Stable
Sea Turtle

Ancient mariners that have been swimming the oceans for over 100 million years.

Oceans Endangered
Chameleon

Master of disguise with color-changing abilities and independently moving eyes.

Africa, Madagascar Vulnerable
Bearded Dragon

Docile lizards known for their distinctive throat display and arm-waving behavior.

Australia Stable
Cobra

Venomous snakes famous for their intimidating hood display and deadly bite.

Africa, Asia Vulnerable

Reptile Facts

Reptiles are over 300 million years old

Snakes can dislocate their jaws to swallow large prey

Some turtles can live over 150 years

Geckos can walk on walls and ceilings
