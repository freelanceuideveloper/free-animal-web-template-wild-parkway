Invertebrates

Explore the diverse world of animals without backbones, from tiny insects to giant squids.

About Invertebrates

Invertebrates make up about 97% of all animal species. They lack backbones but have developed incredible adaptations, from exoskeletons to hydraulic systems, making them incredibly successful.

Butterfly

Beautiful insects that undergo complete metamorphosis, important pollinators with colorful wings.

Worldwide Vulnerable
Octopus

Highly intelligent cephalopods with eight arms, capable of changing color and solving complex problems.

Oceans Stable
Spider

Arachnids that spin silk webs to catch prey, with some species being highly venomous.

Worldwide Stable
Jellyfish

Ancient marine animals with no brain or heart, some species are nearly immortal.

Oceans Stable
Crab

Crustaceans with hard exoskeletons and powerful claws, found in marine and terrestrial environments.

Coasts, Oceans Stable
Starfish

Echinoderms that can regenerate lost limbs and have no brain but complex nervous systems.

Oceans Stable
